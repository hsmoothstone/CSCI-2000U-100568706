(a) echo prints out what you give it to the terminal, in this case, "hello world"
(b) using the curly braces after echo prints each of the contents of the braces (in order) with the rest of the text.
(c) date will print out the current date and time as well as the time zone
(d) whoami will print your username
(e) cal prints a calendar of the year you give it
(f) cal will print only a single month of the year if you give it a month as well as a year. this specific month seems to be missing almost two weeks
(g) bc opens a progamming language "basic calculator" which can be used to do math in shell
(h) piping 5+4 to bc will give bc 5+4 and it will return its value to you, putting echo in front will print it to the terminal
